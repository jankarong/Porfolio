Competitive Analysis

I developed a competitive analysis of Meetup, local platforms, Facebook Events, Eventbrite, and private groups for SportPal. Here are some of the key findings:

Lack of Sports-Specific & Discovery Features

  • Lack of Sports-Specific Focus (5/5)

    General platforms like Meetup and Eventbrite don't focus on sports, making it challenging to locate relevant events.

  • Ineffective Event Discovery (3/5)

    Platforms like Facebook and local sites provide limited search and filtering tools, making it harder to find specific sports events.

  • No Court Booking Feature (4/5)

    These platforms lack options for users to directly book courts, failing to meet the needs of sports enthusiasts who want quick and easy court reservations.

Inefficient Event Management

  • No Save Feature (1/5)

    Users cannot save sports events or activities they're interested in private groups or local platforms, making it inconvenient to revisit and manage preferences.

  • Lack of Unified Event Creation and Payment Support (3/5)

    Event organizers often rely on scattered methods like posts or private groups. This makes editing events tedious, can lead to missing details, and forces payments to be handled offline.

Insufficient Social Features

  • Limited Social Features (2/5)

    Platforms like Eventbrite or local communities don't allow direct interaction or chat, restricting users from building connections within the community.

  • No Share Functionality (2/5)

    Sharing sports events or activities to other platforms, such as private or local platforms, is unsupported, limiting the ability to invite friends or promote events effectively.
