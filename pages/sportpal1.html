Here are some of the key findings :

Lack of Sports-Specific & Discovery Features

  • Lack of Sports-Specific Focus (5/5)

    General platforms like Meetup and Eventbrite don't focus on sports, making it challenging to locate relevant events.

  • Ineffective Event Discovery (3/5)

    Platforms like Facebook and local sites provide limited search and filtering tools, making it harder to find specific sports events.

  • No Court Booking Feature (4/5)

    These platforms lack options for users to directly book courts, failing to meet the needs of sports enthusiasts who want quick and easy court reservations.

Insufficient Social Features

  • Limited Social Features (2/5)

    Platforms like Eventbrite or local communities don't allow direct interaction or chat, restricting users from building connections within the community.

  • No Share Functionality (2/5)

    Sharing sports events or activities to other platforms, such as private or local platforms, is unsupported, limiting the ability to invite friends or promote events effectively.

Inefficient Event Management

  • No Save Feature (1/5)

    Users cannot save sports events or activities they're interested in private groups or local platforms, making it inconvenient to revisit and manage preferences.

  • Lack of Unified Event Creation and Payment Support (3/5)

    Event organizers often rely on scattered methods like posts or private groups. This makes editing events tedious, can lead to missing details, and forces payments to be handled offline.

Positive User Reviews

Key Findings

Positive Feedback

Overall, the app comes across as user-friendly and intuitive. Participants generally found the navigation and interface design to be clear and easy to navigate. The icons and content were well-received, with one person stating they "really enjoyed using the app."

Constructive Feedback

However, a couple minor issues were noted. Some users felt slightly confused by certain navigation features, like the 3/6 tags, when first starting to use the app. Additionally, one participant was frustrated that the price wasn't visible until the final checkout stage,

Iteration

The revised version shows prices more clearly and adds icons to highlight highlight the key info,it can help participants better understand sports information and assist them in quickly joining activities.

I added level categories so users can choose activities based on their skill level,such as beginner or intermediate,which can enhance their activity participation experience.

Fixed a bug for organizers when creating events. Now, they can set visibility to public or private, choose the capacity for the number of participants, and add a level option to better match with suitable participants.

In the attend section of the event details page, I added clear pricing and participant numbers, making the price information less confusing for users and displaying information more clearly.

Takeaways

Study the Design System Early

Spend more time to study the design system and its components, it will save more time later

Build Through Research

Starting with core features proved more effective than building everything at once, allowing for faster launches and user feedback collection.

Keep Users in the Loop

Always take users into account throughout the process to ensure their needs and feedback shape the product effectively.

Design Handoff

We used Zeplin for the handoff process, which helped developers to understand the design flow and view the parameters more clearly and won't destort the design file.
